Quiz 8 Practice Problems

These practice problems cover Lab 7 and Lectures 18-21:

  • confidence intervals for one mean and one proportion,
  • when to use FPC,
  • checking interval conditions,
  • convenience vs simple random sampling (SRS),

  • interpreting confidence intervals in context.

Summary box: interval forms, SE, and conditions

NoteQuick reference for Quiz 8

1) One mean, no FPC (infinite population / tiny sampling fraction)

\[ \bar{x} \pm t^* \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \]

  • Statistic: \(\bar{x}\)
  • SE: \(s/\sqrt{n}\)
  • Conditions: random sample (or SRS), and population roughly normal or \(n\) moderately large

2) One mean, with FPC (finite population, sampling without replacement)

\[ \bar{x} \pm t^* \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}} \sqrt{\frac{N-n}{N-1}} \]

  • Use when population is finite and \(n/N\) is not small.

3) One proportion, no FPC (large-sample normal / Wald form)

\[ \hat{p} \pm z^* \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} \]

  • Statistic: \(\hat{p}=x/n\)
  • SE: \(\sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})/n}\)
  • Conditions: SRS/random sample, independent outcomes, and large counts (\(n\hat{p} \ge 10\), \(n(1-\hat{p}) \ge 10\))

4) One proportion, with FPC

\[ \hat{p} \pm z^* \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}} \sqrt{\frac{N-n}{N-1}} \]

  • Use when sampling without replacement from known finite \(N\) and \(n/N\) is not small.

CI interpretation template (use this wording pattern)

I am 95% confident that the [parameter] in the entire population is between [L] and [U]. In this context, confidence means that if data from many samples were collected the same way I did, then 95% of the intervals computed from these samples would contain the true value of the parameter.

For each scenario:

  1. Identify the population.
  2. Identify the sample.
  3. Identify the parameter.
  4. Identify the statistic (and compute it if enough info is given).

B1.

A random sample of \(n=120\) undergraduates is selected to estimate the proportion who work at least 10 hours/week. In the sample, \(x=46\) say yes.

B2.

A district has \(N=300\) classrooms. An SRS of \(n=30\) classrooms is taken to estimate average number of chairs. The sample mean is \(\bar{x}=29.8\) chairs.

B3.

A clinic wants the proportion of all current patients who are very satisfied. It samples \(n=80\) patients this month, and 52 report “very satisfied.”

B4.

A researcher wants the mean number of minutes/day spent on course websites by all STAT 109 students. She samples 25 students and computes \(\bar{x}=18.4\) minutes.

C2. Registrar list sample

From a full registrar list of all undergraduates (ID 1 to \(N\)), a random-number generator is used to select 120 unique IDs. Those students are surveyed.

C3. Club meeting sample

A researcher surveys 40 students attending the weekly pre-med club meeting to estimate the campus proportion planning to apply to medical school.

C4. SRS with missing frame coverage

An instructor creates a random sample from a list of students with declared majors only; undeclared students are not in the list.

Part E: Identify interval type, FPC, and conditions

For each scenario:

  1. Is the parameter a mean or proportion?
  2. Use FPC or no FPC?
  3. Are the interval conditions reasonably met? (brief justification)

E1.

Sample of \(n=220\) students; \(x=82\) have a part-time job. The sample is SRS from a very large statewide population.

E2.

A district has \(N=300\) classrooms. SRS without replacement gives \(n=30\) classroom chair counts; you want a CI for average chairs/classroom.

E3.

From \(N=80\) patients at a clinic, \(n=50\) are sampled without replacement. Outcome is Yes/No (successful recovery), and you want a CI for the population success proportion.

E4.

SRS of \(n=18\) students from a large campus. Outcome is whether the student is a biology major; observed \(\hat{p}=2/18\).

Part F: Interpret already-calculated intervals in context

For each item, write a correct interpretation sentence in context.

F1. Proportion

A 95% CI for the proportion of all undergraduates who are first-generation college students is \([0.22, 0.30]\).

F2. Mean

A 95% CI for the mean number of minutes/day students spend on course websites is \([41, 55]\) minutes.

F3. Finite population proportion

A school district sampled from a finite list of schools and computed a 95% CI (with FPC) for the proportion of schools older than 50 years: \([0.14, 0.27]\).

F4. Spot the incorrect interpretation

Choose the best interpretation of a 95% CI and explain briefly why:

  • A. “There is a 95% probability that the true parameter is in this specific interval.”
  • B. “If we repeatedly took samples the same way and built intervals this way, about 95% of those intervals would contain the true parameter.”
  • C. “95% of individuals in the population fall between the interval endpoints.”

A researcher wants to estimate the proportion of CA high school students who report having a part-time job.

x <- 80

    1-sample proportions test without continuity correction

95 percent confidence interval:
 0.3029152 0.4290379
sample estimates:
        p 
0.3636364
