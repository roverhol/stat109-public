Lab 3

STAT 109: Introductory Biostatistics

Lab 3: Conditional Probability Simulation

Purpose: This lab uses simulation in R to estimate probabilities involving conditional probability and the multiplication rule. We build on Lab 1–2 skills: sample(), for loops, if statements, vectors, and mean().

Topics:

  • Definition of conditional probability
  • Multiplication rule: \(P(A \text{ and } B) = P(B \mid A)\,P(A)\)
  • Simulation with if statements to count successes
  • Simulating populations with conditional structure

R functions you’ll use: rep(), sample(), for loop, if statement, vector(), sum(), mean(), which()

Part 1: Balls in a bag (drawing without replacement)

A bag contains 3 red and 3 white balls. Two balls are drawn one at a time; the first ball is not replaced before the second is drawn.

1.1 Setup: Create the bag and simulate 10 draws

Your task: Run code to simulate 10 sets of two draws from the bag. Use "R" and "W" for red and white. Store a 1 in successes[k] when the first draw is white and the second is red.

1.2 Explain rep() and balls

Your task: Describe what the vector balls contains. How would you modify it for a bag with 5 red and 2 white balls?

1.3 Explain the if condition

Your task: What does the condition in the if statement specify? When is a “success” recorded?

1.4 Which replicates were successes?

Your task: Use which() to find which replicate numbers had a success.

1.5 Estimate probability with 10,000 replicates

Your task: Set replicates <- 10000 and re-run the simulation. Estimate \(P(\text{white first and red second})\).

1.6 Exactly one red ball

Your task: Modify the simulation to estimate \(P(\text{exactly one red})\) — i.e., either (R then W) or (W then R). Use the logical operator | for “or”.

Part 2: Simulating a population (conditional on sex)

In the US, suppose about 20% of children and 3% of adults like to eat gummy vitamins. Assume a 1:1 child to adult ratio.

Let \(C\) = child, \(G\) = like to eat gummy vitamins.

Questions:

  • What is \(P(C \text{ and } G)\)?
  • What is \(P(G)\)?

R functions you’ll use: sample() with prob, for loop, if statement, sum(), mean()

2.1 Simulate a population of 10,000

Your task: Create vectors child (0 = no, 1 = yes) and gummy (0 = don’t like gummies, 1 = likes gummies). Use if statements so that the probability of liking gummies depends on being a child or not.

2.2 Estimate \(P(C \text{ and } G)\) and \(P(G)\)

Your task: From the simulated population, estimate the probability of being a child and liking gummies, and the probability of liking gummies (overall).

Part 3: Diagnostic test (conditional probability)

A disease has prevalence \(P(D) = 0.01\). A test has sensitivity \(P(\text{positive} \mid D) = 0.95\) and specificity \(P(\text{negative} \mid \text{no } D) = 0.90\).

Your task: Simulate 10,000 individuals with disease status and test result. Estimate \(P(D \mid \text{positive})\) — the probability of having the disease given a positive test.
