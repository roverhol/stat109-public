STAT 109: Introductory Biostatistics — practice problems (binomial test and probability)

Midterm 1 — practice problems

The questions on the in-class portion of the midterm on probability and the binomial test will be drawn from these questions. Please try each question and compare your solutions with your classmates.

1. Dice

Suppose a pair of fair six-sided dice is rolled once.

(a) List the sample space of all possible sums that can result from rolling the pair of dice once.

(b) How many ways can the sum of 7 be obtained when a pair of dice is rolled once?

(c) What is the probability of obtaining a sum of 7 if a fair pair of dice is rolled once?

(d) What is the probability of obtaining a sum of 7 or 11 if a fair pair of dice is rolled once?

2. Screening test (conditional probability)

A university health center is evaluating a screening test for a rare viral infection among students. 4% of students have the infection. If a student has the infection, the test is positive 95% of the time. If a student does not have the infection, the test is negative 90% of the time.

Let:

  • \(D\) = student has the infection
  • \(T^+\) = positive test result

(a) What is the probability that a randomly selected student tests positive?

(b) If a student tests positive, what is the probability that they actually have the infection?

(c) A student tests negative. What is the probability that they truly do not have the infection?

(d) Suppose 1,000 students are tested. How many students would you expect to test positive? Of those who test positive, how many would you expect to actually have the infection?

(e) A student says: “Since the test is 95% accurate, if I test positive there’s a 95% chance I’m infected.” Explain clearly why this statement is incorrect. Your explanation should reference the base rate (prevalence) of the disease.
